## Wealth Architecture Framework

### The Barbell Strategy
```
LEFT SIDE (90%)          │  RIGHT SIDE (10%)
Ultra-safe, boring        │  High-risk, high-reward
─────────────────────────│─────────────────────────
- Cash reserves           │  - Operating business
- Treasuries              │  - Acquisitions
- Broad market indices    │  - New ventures
- Paid-off real estate    │  - Speculative investments
- Insurance (tail risk)   │  - Asymmetric bets
```

### Entity Structure (Evolution Over Time)
```
STAGE 1 (Current — Pre-Revenue):
Founder → LLC/ Sole Proprietorship
         └── Operating Business (simple pass-through)

STAGE 2 (Revenue — $10K+ MRR):
Founder → Holding Company LLC
         ├── Operating Business LLC (each product line separate)
         └── IP Holding LLC (intellectual property)

STAGE 3 (Scale — $100K+ MRR):
Founder → Trust (asset protection)
         └── Holding Company (S-Corp or C-Corp election)
             ├── Operating Subsidiaries (LLCs)
             ├── IP Holding (separate entity)
             ├── Real Estate Holding (separate entity)
             └── Investment Entity (separate entity)

STAGE 4 (Enterprise — $1M+ MRR):
Family Trust → Family Office
              ├── Operating Businesses
              ├── Real Estate Portfolio
              ├── Investment Portfolio
              ├── Venture / Acquisition Arm
              └── Philanthropic Entity
```

## Core Functions

### 1. Asset Protection Design
- Separate risky activities (operations) from valuable assets (IP, real estate)
- Operating entity leases IP from IP holding entity
- Real estate in separate entity from operations
- Each business line in its own LLC (firewall liability)
- Insurance as first line of defense (general liability, E&O, cyber, D&O)

### 2. Tax-Efficient Wealth Building
- Tax-advantaged accounts: SEP IRA, Solo 401k, HSA
- Qualified Small Business Stock (QSBS) — Section 1202 exclusion (up to $10M tax-free)
- 1031 exchanges for real estate (defer capital gains indefinitely)
- Opportunity Zone investments (defer and reduce capital gains)
- Step-up in basis at death (intergenerational wealth transfer)
- Charitable remainder trusts (income + tax deduction + charity)

### 3. Cash Management Architecture
When surplus capital exists:
```
Operating Cash (Checking):
├── 1-2 months of operating expenses
└── Immediate liquidity for payroll, bills

Cash Reserve (High-Yield Savings / Money Market):
├── 3-6 months of operating expenses
└── Never invested, never at risk

Strategic Cash (Brokerage):
├── 6-12 months of expenses
└── Invested conservatively (short-term treasuries, bonds)

Wealth Portfolio (Long-Term):
├── Surplus beyond 12 months
└── Invested for long-term growth
```

### 4. Intergenerational Wealth Planning
- Estate planning: will, trust, power of attorney, healthcare directive
- Gifting strategy: annual gift tax exclusion ($19K/person in 2025)
- Education funding: 529 plans for children/grandchildren
- Legacy planning: what does wealth serve beyond consumption?
- Family governance: how will wealth decisions be made across generations?

### 5. Financial Fortress Principles
1. **Never risk what you need** for what you don't need
2. **Multiple layers** of defense (cash, insurance, entity structure, diversification)
3. **Asymmetric upside** — limited downside, unlimited upside
4. **No single point of failure** in assets, income, or access
5. **Optionality** — cash gives you the ability to act when others can't
