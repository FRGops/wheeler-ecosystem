## 1. INTRODUCTION AND METHODOLOGY

### 1.1 Purpose

This State-by-State Compliance Matrix serves as the operational backbone of the Wheeler Ecosystem surplus funds recovery compliance program. It maps the legal and regulatory landscape for surplus funds recovery across all 50 United States plus the District of Columbia.

### 1.2 Methodology

This matrix was compiled through analysis of:

- State statutory codes (surplus funds, unclaimed property, foreclosure procedures)
- State court rules and procedures
- Case law interpreting surplus funds distribution
- State bar association ethics opinions on finder's fees and referral arrangements
- State regulatory agency guidelines
- Industry standard practices in surplus funds recovery

### 1.3 DISCLAIMER — CRITICAL

> **This document is an operational playbook, NOT legal advice. It does not create an attorney-client relationship. Every state-specific legal conclusion herein is marked with "ATTORNEY REVIEW REQUIRED" and must be verified by licensed counsel in the relevant jurisdiction before being relied upon for any business decision. State laws change frequently. Court rules vary by county within states. Regulatory interpretations evolve. This matrix provides a framework for compliance discussions with qualified attorneys, not a substitute for legal counsel.**

### 1.4 Risk Tier Classification Methodology

States are classified into three tiers based on a composite assessment of:

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Assignment Permissibility | 20% | Can surplus rights be freely assigned to third parties |
| Finder Fee Legality | 20% | Are finder's fees or referral fees explicitly permitted or prohibited |
| Attorney Requirement | 15% | Must a licensed attorney be involved in the claim process |
| Court Filing Restrictions | 15% | Who may file claims and what procedures apply |
| Regulatory Hostility | 15% | Has the state taken enforcement action or issued guidance against surplus recovery |
| Escheat Timeline | 10% | How quickly funds escheat to the state (longer = more favorable) |
| Marketing Restrictions | 5% | Are there restrictions on soliciting claimants |

**Tier 1 (Favorable):** Composite score 75-100 — Clear legal pathways for assignment, no categorical prohibition on finder fees, reasonable court procedures, long escheat periods.

**Tier 2 (Moderate):** Composite score 45-74 — Some restrictions exist but business can operate with enhanced compliance measures. Attorney involvement may be required. Some counties may be more restrictive than others.

**Tier 3 (Restricted):** Composite score 0-44 — Significant restrictions, categorical prohibitions, or hostile regulatory environment. May require structural workarounds or may be non-viable for the standard business model.

## 3. RISK TIER OVERVIEW

### 3.1 Tier 1 — Favorable (35 States + DC partial)

**Characteristics:**
- Surplus funds may be claimed by any interested party including third-party assignees
- No statutory prohibition on finder's fees or assignment of surplus rights
- Court procedures allow pro se filing or filing by assignee with claimant authorization
- Public court records are accessible
- Escheat periods are 3-5+ years providing adequate operational window
- No aggressive regulatory enforcement against surplus recovery industry
- **Attorney review still required** but operational risk is manageable

**Operational Implication:** Standard business model viable with basic compliance infrastructure.

### 3.2 Tier 2 — Moderate (12 States)

**Characteristics:**
- Assignment may be restricted or require court approval in certain circumstances
- Finder fees may require specific disclosures or have implied caps (reasonableness)
- Attorney involvement may be strongly preferred but not statutorily mandated
- Some counties within state may have restrictive local rules
- Court record access may have some restrictions on bulk use
- Disclosure requirements for fee arrangements
- **Attorney review required** — engagement of local counsel strongly recommended

**Operational Implication:** Business model viable with enhanced compliance: local counsel network, robust disclosure forms, court approval where needed.

### 3.3 Tier 3 — Restricted/Prohibited (6 States)

**Characteristics:**
- Assignment of surplus rights may be void or voidable
- Finder's fees may be capped by statute or prohibited as champerty/maintenance
- Attorney required for all court filings (unauthorized practice of law risk)
- Aggressive state bar or financial regulator enforcement
- Short escheat periods limiting operational window
- State-specific regulations that directly target surplus recovery business model
- **ATTORNEY REVIEW MANDATORY** — do not operate without local counsel

**Operational Implication:** Standard business model likely non-viable. Requires alternative structures:
- Full attorney-driven model (attorney engages client directly, fee is attorney's fee)
- Fee-sharing arrangement compliant with state ethics rules
- Referral to in-state attorney network with regulated fee split
- May need to avoid these states entirely depending on risk tolerance

## 7. OPERATIONAL RECOMMENDATIONS BY TIER

### 7.1 Tier 1 Operations (35 States)

**Minimum Compliance Requirements:**
- Written authorization agreement with claimant (notarized recommended)
- Written fee agreement disclosing all fees and the basis for calculation
- Verification of property ownership and foreclosure status
- Court filing in appropriate jurisdiction
- Compliance with unclaimed property reporting deadlines

**Recommended Infrastructure:**
- Standardized authorization and assignment forms vetted by counsel
- County-by-county procedure manual
- Docket monitoring system for new surplus fund postings
- Claim processing workflow with attorney review checkpoints
- Escheat calendar tracking all state deadlines

**Risk Management:**
- Regular attorney review of forms and procedures
- Annual compliance audit
- Consumer complaint monitoring

### 7.2 Tier 2 Operations (12 States)

**Enhanced Compliance Requirements (in addition to Tier 1):**
- **Local counsel engagement:** Retain in-state licensed counsel to review procedures and handle filings
- **Enhanced disclosure:** Fee arrangements must be in writing with specific disclosures required by each state
- **Court approval consideration:** Some assignments or fee arrangements may require court approval
- **Consumer law audit:** Verify compliance with each state's specific consumer protection laws
- **Data use compliance:** Verify permissible use of court records under state rules

**Recommended Infrastructure:**
- State-specific authorization and disclosure forms
- Local counsel network with written engagement agreements
- County-by-county procedure manual including local rules
- Enhanced compliance review before any claimant contact
- Regular compliance calls with local counsel

**Risk Management:**
- Semi-annual compliance audits
- State-by-state regulatory monitoring
- Attorney review of all marketing materials
- Complaint tracking and resolution protocol

### 7.3 Tier 3 Operations (6 States — Conditional Only)

**Requirements for Any Tier 3 Operation:**
> **NOTE: Standard business model should NOT be deployed in Tier 3 states. Only attorney-driven models may be viable.**

**Attorney-Driven Model Requirements:**
- **Licensed in-state attorney** must handle all aspects of the claim
- Attorney directly engages with claimant (not the finder/referral company)
- Attorney's fee is governed by state ethics rules (contingency fee rules, reasonableness)
- Finder company's role limited to:
  - Identifying surplus opportunities through public records research
  - Referring potential claimants to attorneys (with full compliance with referral fee rules)
  - Providing administrative support to attorneys (not directly to claimants)
- **No direct contracting** between finder company and claimant
- **No fee splitting** with non-attorneys (comply with Model Rule 5.4 and state equivalents)
- **No referral fees** paid to non-attorneys (except as permitted by specific state rules)

**Alternative — Referral-Only Model:**
- Company identifies surplus and refers potential claimant to participating attorney
- Attorney handles all legal work
- Attorney pays company a **permissible referral fee** under state ethics rules (some states allow)
- OR: Company provides **marketing services** to attorneys at fair market value (not per-case referral)
- Must document the arrangement as marketing services, not case referral

**Risk Management:**
- Full compliance audit by in-state counsel before commencing operations
- Quarterly compliance reviews
- Regulatory monitoring for enforcement actions
- Exit strategy if regulatory environment becomes hostile
